Ring Size Guide

Method 1: Get a ring that already fits your finger and follow these steps:

_Step 1: Use a ruler with millimeter divisions to measure the inside diameter of the ring

_Step 2: Compare the measured number of mm with the diameter of the rings below.

_Step 3: Your ring number is the number written on the circle.

Method 2

_Step 1: Take scissors, cut a piece of paper 5mm wide (with the tip of chopsticks) and wrap it around your finger.

_Step 2: Take a pen to mark the junction, use scissors to immediately cut the marked part.

_Step 3: Use a ruler to measure the length of the paper sample (For example, measure 55 mm), compare that measurement with the table above.

Attention:

_You should measure 2 to 3 times and slowly and carefully wrap the paper around your finger for accurate measurement results.

_If the weather is cold, your fingers may be smaller than normal, when measuring you should add 1 mm to the circumference. When the weather is hot, it's the opposite, so subtract 1 mm. In case your finger joints are large, you should measure the circumference near the joint (not on the joint), so when wearing the ring it will be easy but not loose.

_With the above simple ways, I hope you can find suitable versions of love rings.


Bracelet Size Guide

Method 1: How to measure bracelet length

Step 1: Use a string (or tape measure/thread/small piece of paper) to wrap around the wrist wearing the bracelet, marking the junction.

Step 2: Measure the length of the string between one junction.

Step 3: Compare the diameter you just measured (in mm) with the ring size table below. Your ring size corresponds to the size number written on the left.
