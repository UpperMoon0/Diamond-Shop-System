About Diamond

  • Diamonds are the hardest naturally occurring substance known to us. Their atoms are arranged in a crystal structure called diamond cubic.
  • This rigid arrangement of carbon atoms gives diamonds their exceptional hardness, making them ideal for cutting and polishing tools.
  • Diamonds are not only prized as gemstones but also have crucial industrial uses. As gemstones, they are popular for their brilliance, fire, and durability. Most gem-grade diamonds are transparent and colorless, although other colors (blue, yellow, pink, etc.) exist due to impurities.
  • In industry, diamonds are used for cutting, grinding, and drilling. Their extreme hardness allows them to withstand intense pressure, making them essential for applications like diamond anvil cells used in scientific research.
